    About the rich land of OKERE DISTRICT

    What is the culture of the people okyere district all about?

    Origins and Heritage: The Okere people are part of the Guan ethnic group, considered some of the earliest settlers in Ghana. Their history is rich with stories of migration and settlement on the Akuapem Ridge. Traditional Governance: Traditional leadership plays a vital role in Okere culture. There are paramount chiefs and queen mothers that hold important positions within the communities. The traditional areas are organized with different towns having their own chiefs, but also being under the okere traditional council. Cultural Significance: Okere has significant historical importance, including its association with Okomfo Anokye, a prominent figure in Ashanti history. They have a rich oral tradition, with stories and traditions passed down through generations. The Okere people have a deep connection to their land, and traditional customs related to land ownership and usage are important. Language and Customs: The primary language spoken among the indigenes is Kyerepon. Traditional customs surrounding marriage, funerals, and other life events are still observed. Influence and Interactions: Okere has a long history of interactions with other ethnic groups, which has influenced their culture. They have played important roles in the history of the region, including forming war alliances.

    Learn About SOME GEOGRAPHIC INFORMATION ON OUR DISTRICT

    Geographic Information:
    Location: The Okere District is situated on the Akuapem Ridge in the southeastern part of the Eastern Region of Ghana. It's approximately 68 kilometers from Accra, Ghana's capital. It shares boundaries with the Akuapem North Municipal Assembly to the south and the Yilo Krobo Municipal Assembly to the east.

    Terrain: The district is characterized by its mountainous terrain, contributing to its cool climate. Key Features: The Akuapem Ridge itself is a significant geographical feature.

    Migration and Travel:
    Historical Migration: The Okere people, part of the Guan ethnic group, have a history of migration to the Akuapem Ridge. Modern Travel:

    Road Transport: The primary means of travel to the Okere District is by road. Roads connect the district to Accra and other parts of the Eastern Region. Vehicles such as buses, "tro-tros" (minibuses), and taxis are commonly used. Travel from Accra is relatively straight forward, with roads leading to the Akuapem ridge. Because of the location on the Akuapem ridge, some roads within the district can be very steep.

    THE MINISTRY OF TOURISM, ART AND CULTURE

    The Ministry of Tourism, Arts and Culture (MoTAC) is to facilitate the interface between government, implementing bodies in tourism, culture, and the creative industries as well as international civil society partners. The Ministry is to develop sustainable tourism pivoted on Ghanaian culture and creative arts as a key to accelerated national development. MoTAC executes its mandate through implementing agencies and currently has thirteen (13) institutions as its main frontline and key implementing agencies
